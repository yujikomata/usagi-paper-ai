TITLE: Comparison of Structure and Viability of Block Cartilage Grafts With Ultrafine Diced Cartilage Grafts in Rabbits.
YEAR: 2025
JOURNAL: 
DOI: 10.1097/gox.0000000000006615
SOURCE: EuropePMC/MED/40078616

BackgroundAutologous block cartilage grafts are traditional and commonly used but have an incidence of warping of 39%. Autologous diced cartilage does not warp or require a long learning curve and can be made from a combination of cartilage sources. Ultrafine diced cartilage (UFDC) (3) facilitates cartilage nutrition owing to its increased surface area and reduced diffusion distance. An ultrafine size reduces the risk of irregular graft contours.MethodsRandomized pre- and posttest experimental studies on rabbits (Oryctolagus cuniculus). The fixed variables were block and ultrafine diced cartilage grafts. The dependent variables were structure, viability, and collagen content 1 month after implantation.ResultsThere was significant warping of the block cartilage postimplantation (P value 0.000 ConclusionsUFDC displayed better microscopic viability and higher collagen content than the block cartilage. The structural and functional integrity of the UFDC presents great potential for use in the plastic surgery armamentarium.
