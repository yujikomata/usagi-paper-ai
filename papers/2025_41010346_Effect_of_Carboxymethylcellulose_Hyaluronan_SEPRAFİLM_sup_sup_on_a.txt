Purpose: This study aimed to evaluate the efficacy of carboxymethylcellulose (Seprafilm®) for the prevention and treatment of arthrofibrosis in rabbit knees, as well as to investigate its underlying mechanisms of action against fibrosis and adhesion formation. Methods: Sixteen male New Zealand white rabbits were randomly divided into two groups: a control group and a treatment group that received Seprafilm® following surgically induced arthrofibrosis in the knee joint. Macroscopic and histological assessments were performed to evaluate adhesion, fibrosis, inflammation, and edema. Results: In the control group, macroscopic adhesion was severe in five rabbits (62.5%), moderate in two (25%), and minimal in one (12.5%). No macroscopic adhesion was observed in the Seprafilm® group.
